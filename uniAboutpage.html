ABOUT HOWARD

Brief History

Founded on March 2nd 1867, in Washington, D.C

*Early Years (1867-1920s):*
- Founded by General Oliver O. Howard and the First Congregational Society to provide education for African Americans after the Civil War
- Initially offered theological, medical, and teacher training programs
- Played a crucial role in educating freed slaves and African Americans during the Reconstruction Era

*Growth and Development (1920s-1960s):*
- Expanded academic programs, including law, medicine, and arts and sciences - Became a hub for African American culture, intellectualism, and activism - Attracted prominent faculty and students, including notable alumni like Thurgood Marshall and Toni Morrison

*Civil Rights Era (1950s-1970s):*
- Howard University students and faculty actively participated in the Civil Rights Movement, advocating for social justice and equality - The university's law school played a significant role in shaping civil rights law

*Modern Era (1980s-present):*
- Continued to grow and expand academic programs, research initiatives, and community engagement - Maintained its reputation as a premier historically black college or university (HBCU) - Remained a leader in producing graduates who go on to make significant contributions in various fields Throughout its history, Howard University has remained committed to academic excellence, social justice, and empowering African American students and communities.
